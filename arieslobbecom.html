Statistical Computing Project

This was the final project of the course Statistical Computing and Machine Learning which I took in the fall of 2015 with professor Daniel Kaplan. Anna Winkler and I trained various tree based methods on a classic data set from the UC Irvine collection, and we compared the out-of-sample prediction error of the tree methods with those of various other methods that have been presented in the literature.

All Pairs Shortest Path Project

I spent the summer of 2015 doing research under prof. Henning Meyerhenke and Elisabetta Bergamini with the Parallel Computing Group at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology. I worked on a fundamental and well studied problem in graph algorithms called the dynamic all pairs shortest path problem. We came up with a new and improved algorithm for the incremental variant of the problem. I implemented and tested the algorithm, proved its correctness, and presented it at an undergraduate research symposium. My co-authors and I are presenting our ideas in a paper.
